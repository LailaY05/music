Death Metal

What exactly is death metal?

Death metal is a genre of metal characterized by low-tuned guitars, deeper/lower-toned vocals, and lots of gutturals. You may know bands like Cannibal Corpse or Blood Incantation.

A lot of these bands are infamous for their disturbing themes, whether they're found in the band's name, the song titles, or album covers. Just for your (and my grade's) sake, I will not be elaborating on such disturbing themes.

There is a subgenre of death metal called deathcore that I listen to a bit more than death metal.

Deathcore is a fusion of death metal and heavy metal, metalcore, hardcore, or post-hardcore elements. It's a little difficult to explain, but think of it this way: deathcore is mostly just death metal but with breakdowns. Source: A conversation I had while I was working on this project

Also, some deathcore vocalists like Trevor Strnad of The Black Dahlia Murder don't always use lower-toned vocals.

So, what are some good death metal bands for starters?

Well, as mentioned before, Death is a good band. It's not exactly death metal, but rather thrashy death metal. I recommend and Leprosy. These do NOT have typical low-toned vocals or gutturals, but it's a good place to start if you're new to this genre.

If you want something heavier, Hostage by Chelsea Grin is one of my all-time favorite deathcore songs. The album that it comes from is great in general, but this song is one of the best (in my opinion). If you want something heavier; something that's actually pure death metal, Zombie Apocalypse (this is the alternative name) by Mortician or Fixated on Devastation by Dying Fetus would be good places to start. And yes, I am aware of the names.
