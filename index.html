What exactly is death metal?

Death metal is a genre of metal characterized by low-tuned guitars, deeper/lower-toned vocals, and lots of gutturals. You may know bands like Cannibal Corpse, Death or Devourment.

A lot of these bands are infamous for their disturbing themes, whether they're found in the band's name, the band's song titles, or the band's album covers. Just for your (and my grade's) sake, I will not be elaborating on such disturbing themes.

While I don't listen to too much death metal, I do listen to a lot of deathcore

Deathcore is a fusion of death metal and heavy metal, metalcore, or post-hardcore elements. It's a little difficult to explain, but think of it this way: deathcore is mostly just death metal but with breakdowns.

Also, some deathcore vocalists like Trevor Strnad of The Black Dahlia Murder don't always use lower-toned vocals, but are still deathcore.

So, what are some good death metal bands for starters?

Well, as mentioned before, Death is a good band. It's not exactly death metal, but rather thrashy death metal. I recommend and Leprosy. These do NOT have typical low-toned vocals or gutturals, but it's a good place to start if you're new to this genre.

If you want something heavier, Hostage by Chelsea Grin is one of my all-time favorite deathcore songs. The album that it comes from is great in general, but this song is one of the best (in my opinion). If you want something heavier; something that's actually pure death metal, Zombie Apocalypse (this is the alternative name) by Mortician or Fixated on Devastation by Dying Fetus would be good places to start.
